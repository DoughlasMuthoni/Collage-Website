Preamble

The Examination Department is a vital cornerstone of academic excellence and integrity within our institution. Our mission is to ensure the fair, efficient, and transparent administration of all assessments, fostering a conducive environment for evaluating and enhancing student performance.

The Examination Department operates as a guardian of academic standards, ensuring that the principles of fairness, accountability, and excellence guide all our activities. In doing so, we aim to contribute to the intellectual and professional advancement of our students and uphold the credibility of the institution.

With a focus on innovation, professionalism, and continuous improvement, we endeavor to adapt to evolving educational needs while maintaining the trust and confidence of all stakeholders in the assessment process.

  • Assessing whether educational goals have been achieved.
  • Self-evaluation for both lecturer and student.
  • Improvement of the learning process and the learning environment.
  • Placement and progression for the learner.

We are committed to upholding the highest standards of academic integrity by implementing robust policies, secure examination procedures, and equitable practices. Through collaboration with faculty, students, and administrative teams, we strive to maintain accuracy in assessment processes and provide timely feedback that supports student growth and development.

Vision

To be a leading center of excellence in technical and vocational education, producing globally competitive graduates who drive progress and innovation.

Mission

To empower individuals through comprehensive technical training and education that meets the demands of a dynamic and evolving job market while fostering innovation, professionalism, and community engagement..

Grading System +

The grading system is as follows:

  • A: Excellent (80-100%)
  • B: Very Good (70-79%)
  • C: Good (60-69%)
  • D: Satisfactory (50-59%)
  • F: Fail (Below 50%)
Punishable Offenses +

Engaging in any of the following is considered a punishable offense:

  • Cheating during examinations.
  • Plagiarism in assignments or projects.
  • Disruptive behavior in class or examination halls.
  • Failure to adhere to institutional policies and procedures.
